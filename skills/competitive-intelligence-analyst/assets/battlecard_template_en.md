## Objection Handling

### Objection 1: "[Competitor] is cheaper"

**Acknowledge**:
"You're right that their upfront cost appears lower"

**Reframe**:
"However, many customers find that total cost of ownership is what matters"

**Prove**:
"For example, [Customer] reduced their 3-year TCO by 20% with us"

**Confirm**:
"Would you like to do a similar comparison for your situation?"

---

### Objection 2: "[Competitor] has more features"

**Acknowledge**:
"It's true they have more features on paper"

**Reframe**:
"But how many of those features will you actually use?"

**Prove**:
"[Customer] improved productivity by 30% with our simpler solution"

**Confirm**:
"Should we identify the features you really need together?"

---

### Objection 3: "[Competitor] has more customers"

**Acknowledge**:
"They do have an impressive customer list"

**Reframe**:
"But customers in your specific industry and size matter most"

**Prove**:
"We have great success stories from [similar customer]"

**Confirm**:
"Would you like me to share relevant case studies?"
