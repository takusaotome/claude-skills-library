# ISO High-Level Structure (HLS) - Annex SL

## Overview

The High-Level Structure (HLS), defined in ISO/IEC Directives Part 1, Annex SL, provides a common framework for all ISO management system standards. This ensures consistency and facilitates integration of multiple management systems.

## 10-Clause Structure

All ISO management system standards follow this identical structure:

### Clause 1: Scope
- Defines the boundaries of the management system
- Specifies intended outcomes
- Exclusions and their justification

### Clause 2: Normative References
- Referenced documents indispensable for application
- Dated references: specific edition applies
- Undated references: latest edition applies

### Clause 3: Terms and Definitions
- Standard-specific terminology
- Common definitions across ISO standards
- Context-specific interpretations

### Clause 4: Context of the Organization
- **4.1** Understanding the organization and its context
  - Internal issues: values, culture, knowledge, performance
  - External issues: legal, technological, competitive, market, cultural
- **4.2** Understanding needs and expectations of interested parties
  - Identify relevant interested parties
  - Determine their relevant requirements
- **4.3** Determining the scope of the management system
  - Boundaries and applicability
  - Document and maintain scope
- **4.4** Management system and its processes
  - Establish, implement, maintain, and continually improve
  - Determine processes and their interactions

### Clause 5: Leadership
- **5.1** Leadership and commitment
  - Top management accountability
  - Policy establishment
  - Resource provision
  - Engagement and support of people
- **5.2** Policy
  - Appropriate to purpose
  - Framework for objectives
  - Commitment to requirements
  - Commitment to continual improvement
- **5.3** Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities
  - Assign and communicate responsibilities
  - Ensure system conformity
  - Report on performance

### Clause 6: Planning
- **6.1** Actions to address risks and opportunities
  - Identify risks and opportunities
  - Plan actions to address them
  - Integrate into processes
  - Evaluate effectiveness
- **6.2** Objectives and planning to achieve them
  - Establish objectives at relevant functions
  - Consistent with policy
  - Measurable (if practicable)
  - Monitored, communicated, updated
- **6.3** Planning of changes
  - Consider purpose and consequences
  - Ensure integrity of management system
  - Resource availability
  - Responsibility allocation

### Clause 7: Support
- **7.1** Resources
  - General: determine and provide resources
  - People: competent persons
  - Infrastructure: buildings, equipment, IT
  - Environment: physical, social, psychological
  - Monitoring and measuring resources (calibration)
  - Organizational knowledge
- **7.2** Competence
  - Determine required competence
  - Ensure competence through education, training, experience
  - Take actions to acquire competence
  - Retain documented evidence
- **7.3** Awareness
  - Policy awareness
  - Contribution to effectiveness
  - Implications of nonconformity
- **7.4** Communication
  - Internal and external communications
  - What, when, whom, how to communicate
- **7.5** Documented information
  - General requirements
  - Creating and updating
  - Control of documented information

### Clause 8: Operation
- **8.1** Operational planning and control
  - Plan, implement, control processes
  - Determine requirements for products/services
  - Establish criteria for processes
  - Implement control according to criteria
  - Determine and maintain documented information
  - Control planned changes
  - Control unintended changes

### Clause 9: Performance Evaluation
- **9.1** Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation
  - Determine what to monitor and measure
  - Methods for valid results
  - When to monitor and measure
  - When to analyze and evaluate
  - Evaluate management system effectiveness
- **9.2** Internal audit
  - Conduct at planned intervals
  - Conform to organization's own requirements
  - Conform to standard requirements
  - Effectively implemented and maintained
- **9.3** Management review
  - Review at planned intervals
  - Consider status of actions from previous reviews
  - Consider changes in external/internal issues
  - Consider performance and effectiveness
  - Consider adequacy of resources
  - Consider opportunities for improvement

### Clause 10: Improvement
- **10.1** General
  - Determine opportunities for improvement
  - Implement necessary actions
- **10.2** Nonconformity and corrective action
  - React to nonconformity
  - Evaluate need for action to eliminate cause
  - Implement corrective action
  - Review effectiveness
  - Update risks and opportunities if needed
  - Make changes to management system if needed
- **10.3** Continual improvement
  - Continually improve suitability, adequacy, effectiveness

## Standard-Specific Additions

Each ISO standard adds specific requirements to the HLS:

| Standard | Key Additions |
|----------|--------------|
| ISO 9001 | Customer focus, product/service requirements, design, production |
| ISO 27001 | Information security risk assessment, Statement of Applicability, Annex A controls |
| ISO 22301 | Business impact analysis, business continuity strategies, exercises |
| ISO 45001 | Worker participation, hazard identification, emergency preparedness |
| ISO 14001 | Environmental aspects, compliance obligations, life cycle perspective |

## Integration Benefits

Using HLS enables:
1. **Reduced duplication**: Common elements addressed once
2. **Simplified audits**: Combined audits possible
3. **Consistent terminology**: Easier communication
4. **Efficient resources**: Single management system team
5. **Streamlined documentation**: Common procedures

## Compliance Scoring Guide

When assessing compliance against any clause:

| Score | Level | Description | Evidence |
|-------|-------|-------------|----------|
| 5 | Fully Compliant | Requirement fully implemented and effective | Complete records, consistent practice |
| 4 | Mostly Compliant | Minor gaps in implementation or evidence | Most records available, occasional gaps |
| 3 | Partially Compliant | Significant gaps but some implementation | Limited records, inconsistent practice |
| 2 | Minimally Compliant | Major gaps, early implementation | Few records, awareness exists |
| 1 | Not Compliant | No implementation | No records or evidence |
| 0 | Not Applicable | Requirement excluded from scope | Justified exclusion documented |
