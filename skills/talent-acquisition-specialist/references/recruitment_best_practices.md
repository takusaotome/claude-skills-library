# Recruitment Best Practices Guide

## Overview

This guide covers modern recruitment best practices including employer branding, candidate experience, sourcing strategies, and key metrics. Use this reference when planning and executing recruitment activities.

## Employer Branding

### What is Employer Brand?

Employer brand is the reputation and image of an organization as an employer. It encompasses:

- **Employee Value Proposition (EVP)**: What employees receive in return for their work
- **Culture**: How work gets done, values, and behaviors
- **Perception**: How candidates and employees view the company

### Building Employer Brand

| Element | Actions |
|---------|---------|
| **Define EVP** | Identify unique benefits, culture, growth opportunities |
| **Tell Stories** | Share employee testimonials, day-in-the-life content |
| **Be Present** | Active on LinkedIn, Glassdoor, career sites |
| **Engage Employees** | Turn employees into brand ambassadors |
| **Deliver Consistently** | Align promise with actual experience |

### Employer Brand Channels

| Channel | Purpose | Best Practices |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Career Website | Central hub for employer brand | Modern design, easy navigation, mobile-friendly |
| LinkedIn | Professional networking | Regular posts, employee spotlights, company updates |
| Glassdoor | Review platform | Respond to reviews, encourage employee feedback |
| Social Media | Broader reach | Authentic content, behind-the-scenes |
| Job Postings | First impression | Clear, engaging, reflects culture |

## Candidate Experience

### Candidate Journey Stages

```
Awareness → Consideration → Application → Selection → Offer → Onboarding
```

### Touchpoints and Best Practices

| Stage | Touchpoints | Best Practices |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Awareness** | Job ads, career site, social | Clear branding, compelling messaging |
| **Consideration** | Company research, reviews | Authentic content, employee testimonials |
| **Application** | Application form, ATS | Simple process, mobile-friendly, < 15 min |
| **Selection** | Interviews, assessments | Respectful scheduling, preparation materials |
| **Offer** | Offer call, documentation | Prompt decision, personal touch |
| **Onboarding** | Pre-boarding, Day 1 | Warm welcome, clear expectations |

### Communication Best Practices

| Timing | Standard |
|--------|----------|
| Application received | Immediate auto-confirmation |
| Screening decision | Within 1 week |
| Interview scheduling | Within 48 hours of decision |
| Post-interview update | Within 3 business days |
| Offer decision | Within 1 week of final interview |
| Rejection notification | Same day as decision |

### Rejection Best Practices

1. **Be Timely** - Don't leave candidates waiting
2. **Be Personal** - Use candidate's name, reference the role
3. **Be Grateful** - Thank them for their time and interest
4. **Be Honest** - Provide general feedback when possible
5. **Be Encouraging** - Invite future applications if appropriate

**Example Rejection Template:**

```
Subject: Your Application for [Role] at [Company]

Dear [Name],

Thank you for your interest in the [Role] position and for taking the time
to interview with our team.

After careful consideration, we have decided to move forward with other
candidates whose experience more closely aligns with our current needs.

We encourage you to apply for future openings that match your background.

Best regards,
[Recruiter Name]
```

## Sourcing Strategies

### Sourcing Channel Comparison

| Channel | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|---------|------|------|----------|
| **Employee Referrals** | High quality, culture fit, faster hire | Limited pool, homogeneity risk | All levels |
| **LinkedIn** | Large pool, passive candidates | Competitive, expensive | Mid-senior |
| **Job Boards** | High volume | Low quality ratio | Entry-mid level |
| **Agencies** | Specialized expertise, speed | High cost | Executive, niche |
| **Campus Recruiting** | Pipeline building | Seasonal, inexperienced | Entry level |
| **Networking Events** | Personal connection | Time-intensive | All levels |

### Employee Referral Program

**Program Elements:**

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Bonus Structure | $2,000-$10,000 depending on role level |
| Payment Timing | 50% at hire, 50% after probation |
| Communication | Regular reminders, success stories |
| Process | Simple submission, status updates |
| Recognition | Public thanks, referrer spotlights |

### Passive Candidate Outreach

**Outreach Message Elements:**

1. **Personalization** - Reference specific work or background
2. **Value Proposition** - Why this opportunity matters
3. **Social Proof** - Company achievements, team quality
4. **Call to Action** - Clear next step (call, chat)

**Example Outreach:**

```
Subject: [Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out

Hi [Name],

I noticed your work on [Specific Project] and was impressed by [Specific Achievement].

At [Company], we're building [Brief Description] and looking for someone with
your expertise in [Skill Area]. We offer [Key EVP Elements].

Would you be open to a quick call to learn more?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Diversity & Inclusion in Recruiting

### D&I Best Practices

| Area | Actions |
|------|---------|
| **Job Descriptions** | Remove gendered language, focus on essential requirements |
| **Sourcing** | Partner with diverse organizations, expand networks |
| **Screening** | Blind resume review, structured criteria |
| **Interviewing** | Diverse panels, structured questions, bias training |
| **Offers** | Pay equity analysis, consistent terms |

### Inclusive Language Examples

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "Manpower" | "Workforce" |
| "Guys" | "Team" |
| "He/She" | "They" |
| "Ninja/Rockstar" | "Expert" |
| "Culture fit" | "Values alignment" |

## Recruitment Metrics

### Key Performance Indicators

| Metric | Formula | Target | Purpose |
|--------|---------|--------|---------|
| **Time to Fill** | Days from req open to start | < 45 days | Process efficiency |
| **Time to Hire** | Days from first contact to offer accept | < 30 days | Candidate experience |
| **Cost per Hire** | Total cost / hires | Industry benchmark | Budget management |
| **Quality of Hire** | Avg performance rating at 12 months | > 3.5/5 | Hiring effectiveness |
| **Offer Acceptance Rate** | Accepted / Total offers | > 85% | Competitiveness |
| **Source of Hire** | % by channel | Varies | Channel optimization |
| **Application Completion Rate** | Completed / Started | > 70% | Process friction |
| **Candidate Net Promoter Score** | Survey score | > 50 | Candidate experience |

## Compliance and Legal

### Required Documentation

| Document | Retention Period |
|----------|-----------------|
| Job postings | 1 year |
| Applications | 1-2 years |
| Interview notes | 1-2 years |
| Offer letters | Duration of employment + 5 years |
| Background checks | 5 years (varies by jurisdiction) |

### EEOC Compliance (US)

1. Track applicant demographics (voluntary)
2. Document selection criteria and decisions
3. Ensure consistent process for all candidates
4. Maintain records for 1-2 years
5. Accommodate disabilities in the hiring process

## Common Recruitment Challenges

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| **Low application volume** | Improve job posting SEO, expand sourcing channels |
| **High drop-off** | Simplify application, add save progress |
| **Poor candidate quality** | Refine requirements, improve screening |
| **Slow process** | Audit bottlenecks, set SLAs |
| **Offer declines** | Improve EVP, benchmark compensation |
| **Hiring manager delays** | Set expectations, provide scheduling tools |
| **Ghosting candidates** | Improve communication, set auto-reminders |

## Best Practices Summary

### Do's

1. **Treat candidates like customers** - Their experience matters
2. **Move quickly** - Speed wins in competitive markets
3. **Communicate proactively** - Never leave candidates guessing
4. **Use data** - Let metrics guide decisions
5. **Collaborate** - Align with hiring managers closely
6. **Build pipeline** - Continuous sourcing, not just when hiring
7. **Measure and improve** - Regular process audits

### Don'ts

1. **Don't ghost candidates** - Always close the loop
2. **Don't oversell** - Honest expectations reduce turnover
3. **Don't skip references** - They provide valuable insight
4. **Don't delay decisions** - Top candidates move fast
5. **Don't ignore feedback** - Candidate input improves process
