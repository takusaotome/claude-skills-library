# IIA International Standards Quick Reference

## Purpose

This document provides a quick reference to the IIA (Institute of Internal Auditors) International Standards for the Professional Practice of Internal Auditing.

---

## Definition of Internal Auditing

> Internal auditing is an independent, objective assurance and consulting activity designed to add value and improve an organization's operations. It helps an organization accomplish its objectives by bringing a systematic, disciplined approach to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes.

## Core Concepts

### Three Lines Model (formerly Three Lines of Defense)

1. **First Line**: Operational management (owns and manages risks)
2. **Second Line**: Risk management and compliance functions (oversight)
3. **Third Line**: Internal audit (independent assurance)

### Risk-Based Internal Auditing (RBIA)

- Focus audit resources on areas of highest risk
- Align audit plan with organizational objectives
- Update risk assessment periodically
- Communicate risk insights to management and board

### Internal Audit Charter

Required elements:
- Purpose, authority, and responsibility of internal audit
- Independence and objectivity requirements
- Functional reporting to audit committee
- Administrative reporting relationships
- Access to records, personnel, physical properties
- Scope of internal audit activities

## Audit Opinion Framework

| Opinion | Description | Criteria |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **Satisfactory** | Controls adequately designed and operating effectively | No significant weaknesses; minor issues only |
| **Needs Improvement** | Control weaknesses require management attention | Moderate risk exposure; timely remediation needed |
| **Unsatisfactory** | Significant control deficiencies exist | High risk of material impact; immediate action required |

---

## Quality Attributes of Findings

Well-developed findings include:

1. **Condition**: What is the current state? (What we found)
2. **Criteria**: What should be the state? (Standard, policy, best practice)
3. **Cause**: Why did the issue occur? (Root cause)
4. **Effect**: What is the impact or risk? (Consequence)
5. **Recommendation**: What should be done? (Action)
