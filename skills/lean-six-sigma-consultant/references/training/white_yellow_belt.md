# White Belt and Yellow Belt Training Curriculum

## Overview

White Belt and Yellow Belt levels provide foundational understanding of Lean Six Sigma concepts. These are entry-level certifications suitable for all employees.

## White Belt Curriculum

### Target Audience
- All employees
- New hires orientation
- Executives seeking overview
- Support function staff

### Training Duration
- 2-4 hours total
- Can be self-paced or instructor-led

### Learning Objectives

After completing White Belt training, participants will be able to:
1. Explain what Lean Six Sigma is and its benefits
2. Describe the DMAIC methodology
3. Identify the 8 wastes (DOWNTIME)
4. Understand basic quality concepts
5. Support improvement projects as a team member

### Module 1: Introduction to Lean Six Sigma (45 min)

**Topics**:
- History: Toyota Production System and Motorola
- What is Lean? Focus on waste elimination
- What is Six Sigma? Focus on variation reduction
- Why integrate? Lean Six Sigma synergy
- Benefits: Quality, speed, cost, customer satisfaction

**Key Concepts**:
- **Lean**: Maximize value, minimize waste
- **Six Sigma**: 3.4 defects per million opportunities
- **Variation**: Enemy of quality
- **Continuous Improvement**: Never stop improving

**Activity**:
- Video case study of successful LSS implementation
- Discussion: Where do you see waste in your area?

### Module 2: Understanding Waste (45 min)

**Topics**:
- Definition of waste vs. value-add
- The 8 Wastes (DOWNTIME):
  - **D**efects
  - **O**verproduction
  - **W**aiting
  - **N**on-utilized talent
  - **T**ransportation
  - **I**nventory
  - **M**otion
  - **E**xtra processing

**Activity**:
- Waste walk simulation or video
- Identify wastes in familiar process
- Group discussion

### Module 3: DMAIC Overview (30 min)

**Topics**:
- D - Define: What's the problem?
- M - Measure: How big is it?
- A - Analyze: Why is it happening?
- I - Improve: How do we fix it?
- C - Control: How do we keep it fixed?

**Key Points**:
- Data-driven decision making
- Root cause focus (not symptoms)
- Structured problem solving

### Module 4: Your Role in LSS (30 min)

**Topics**:
- How projects work
- Team roles and responsibilities
- How to contribute ideas
- Suggestion systems
- Supporting project teams

**Takeaways**:
- Everyone can contribute
- Report problems and ideas
- Participate in improvement activities
- Support changes with positive attitude

### Assessment
- 10-question quiz (70% passing)
- Topics covered in training

## Yellow Belt Curriculum

### Target Audience
- Front-line employees
- Team leads and supervisors
- Administrative staff
- Project team members

### Training Duration
- 1-2 days (8-16 hours)
- Instructor-led recommended
- May include simulation exercise

### Learning Objectives

After completing Yellow Belt training, participants will be able to:
1. Apply basic Lean Six Sigma tools
2. Participate effectively on project teams
3. Collect and present data
4. Lead small improvement activities
5. Use simple problem-solving tools

### Module 1: Lean Six Sigma Foundations (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Review of White Belt concepts
- Voice of the Customer (VOC)
- Critical to Quality (CTQ)
- Cost of Poor Quality (COPQ)
- Project selection basics

**Deeper Understanding**:
- Customer requirements drive improvement
- Quality has direct cost impact
- Linking business goals to projects

### Module 2: Define Phase Tools (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Problem statements
  - Specific, measurable, data-based
  - What, when, where, how much
  - Avoid causes and solutions
- Goal statements (SMART)
- Scope definition (is/is not)
- SIPOC diagrams

**SIPOC Practice**:
| S | I | P | O | C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Suppliers | Inputs | Process Steps | Outputs | Customers |

**Activity**: Create SIPOC for familiar process

### Module 3: Measure Phase Tools (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Data types
  - Continuous (measurements)
  - Discrete/Attribute (counts, categories)
- Data collection basics
  - Operational definitions
  - Check sheets
  - Sampling concepts
- Basic charts
  - Run charts
  - Bar charts
  - Pie charts

**Activity**: Design a data collection plan for workplace issue

### Module 4: Analyze Phase Tools (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Pareto analysis (80/20 rule)
- Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram
  - 6M categories (Man, Machine, Method, Material, Measurement, Mother Nature)
- 5 Whys technique
- Process mapping
  - Flowcharts
  - Identifying value-add vs. non-value-add

**Fishbone Practice**:
```
         ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
         │                                                 │
   Man───┼───Machine                                       │
         │                                                 │
         │                           ─────────────► EFFECT │
         │                                                 │
Method───┼───Material                                      │
         │                                                 │
         └─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Activity**: Create fishbone diagram for common problem

### Module 5: Improve and Control Basics (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Solution brainstorming
- Impact/Effort matrix
- 5S workplace organization
  1. Sort (Seiri)
  2. Set in order (Seiton)
  3. Shine (Seiso)
  4. Standardize (Seiketsu)
  5. Sustain (Shitsuke)
- Standard work basics
- Visual management

**5S Practice**:
- Sort exercise with sample items
- Organize workspace simulation

### Module 6: Team Dynamics and Project Support (2 hours)

**Topics**:
- Team roles (Leader, Member, Champion, Facilitator)
- Meeting effectiveness
- Presentation skills
- Change management basics
- Communication strategies

**Activity**: Role-play project team meeting

### Simulation Exercise (Optional - 2-4 hours)

**Options**:
- Paper airplane factory
- Dice game
- LEGO simulation
- Process simulation

**Learning Points**:
- See waste in action
- Experience DMAIC application
- Practice teamwork

### Assessment

**Requirements for Certification**:
- Attend all training sessions
- Pass written exam (70% or higher)
- Complete simulation exercise
- Optional: Small improvement project participation

**Exam Topics**:
- DMAIC phases and deliverables
- Waste identification
- Basic tools (SIPOC, fishbone, Pareto, 5 Whys)
- Data collection concepts
- 5S principles

## Comparison: White Belt vs. Yellow Belt

| Aspect | White Belt | Yellow Belt |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Duration | 2-4 hours | 1-2 days |
| Depth | Awareness | Working knowledge |
| Tools | Waste identification | SIPOC, Fishbone, Pareto, 5 Whys, 5S |
| Role | Support projects | Active team member |
| Projects | None required | May lead small improvements |
| Exam | Basic quiz | Comprehensive exam |

## Recommended Resources

### Books
- "The Lean Six Sigma Pocket Toolbook" - Michael L. George
- "Lean Thinking" - Womack & Jones (introduction chapters)

### Videos/Online
- ASQ (American Society for Quality) introductory courses
- Gemba Academy free content
- Company-specific training materials

### Practice Activities

**For White Belts**:
- Daily waste identification journal
- Participate in 5S event
- Attend project tollgate review

**For Yellow Belts**:
- Lead departmental Kaizen
- Create SIPOC for main process
- Conduct 5 Whys for recurring problem
- Support Green Belt project

## Career Path

```
White Belt
    ↓
Yellow Belt
    ↓
Green Belt (leads projects part-time)
    ↓
Black Belt (leads projects full-time)
    ↓
Master Black Belt (trains and mentors)
```

## Certification Maintenance

**Recommended**:
- Annual refresher training
- Participate in at least one improvement activity per year
- Stay connected to LSS community
- Share learnings with colleagues
