# Black Belt Training Curriculum

## Overview

Black Belt certification represents the highest level of practical Lean Six Sigma expertise for project leaders. Black Belts are full-time improvement professionals who lead complex projects, mentor Green Belts, and drive organizational transformation.

## Target Audience

- Green Belts with proven project success (2+ completed)
- Individuals transitioning to full-time improvement roles
- Engineers and technical professionals
- Quality managers and directors
- Operations leaders

## Prerequisites

- Green Belt certification with completed project
- Strong analytical and statistical background
- Demonstrated leadership capability
- Management sponsorship for Black Belt role

## Training Duration

- 20 days (160 hours) of classroom training
- Typically delivered in 4-5 week-long sessions over 4-6 months
- 6-12 months to complete certification requirements
- 2-3 projects during certification

## Learning Objectives

After completing Black Belt training and projects, participants will be able to:
1. Lead complex, cross-functional DMAIC and DMADV projects
2. Apply advanced statistical analysis and interpretation
3. Coach and mentor Green Belts and project teams
4. Facilitate organizational change
5. Develop project pipeline and prioritize opportunities
6. Link projects to strategic business objectives
7. Train Green Belts and Yellow Belts

## Week 2: Advanced Measure and MSA

### Day 1-2: Advanced Measurement System Analysis

**Topics**:
- Gage R&R (crossed and nested studies)
- Linearity and bias studies
- Attribute agreement analysis
- Kappa statistics
- Measurement system improvement
- Continuous gage studies

**MSA Acceptance Criteria**:
| %R&R | NDC | Acceptability |
|------|-----|---------------|
| < 10% | > 5 | Excellent |
| 10-20% | 3-5 | Acceptable for process decisions |
| 20-30% | 2-3 | Marginal, improvement needed |
| > 30% | < 2 | Unacceptable |

## Week 3: Advanced Analyze

### Day 1-2: Advanced Hypothesis Testing

**Topics**:
- Choosing the right test
- Sample size determination
- Power analysis
- Multiple comparison procedures
- Non-parametric tests
  - Mann-Whitney
  - Kruskal-Wallis
  - Mood's median
- Proportion tests
- Chi-square applications

**Test Selection Guide**:
| Comparison | Data Type | Test |
|------------|-----------|------|
| 2 means, independent | Continuous | 2-sample t |
| 2 means, paired | Continuous | Paired t |
| 3+ means | Continuous | ANOVA |
| 2 proportions | Attribute | 2 proportion |
| 3+ proportions | Attribute | Chi-square |

### Day 3-4: Regression Analysis

## Project Requirements

### Certification Requirements

1. **Complete training program** (all weeks, all assessments)
2. **Pass Black Belt examination** (minimum 80%)
3. **Complete primary project** (complex, cross-functional, significant impact)
4. **Mentor at least one Green Belt** through their project
5. **Present projects** to certification committee
6. **Champion and executive sign-off**

### Primary Project Characteristics

- Cross-functional scope
- Significant financial impact (varies by organization)
- Complex problem requiring advanced tools
- 6-12 month duration
- Multiple stakeholders

### Secondary Activities

- Support additional projects
- Mentor Green Belt project
- Participate in project reviews
- Contribute to LSS community

## Examination

### Format
- 150-200 questions
- 4-6 hours
- Mix of multiple choice and calculation
- Open book (for some certifications)
- Scenario-based questions

### Topics and Weighting

| Topic | Approximate Weight |
|-------|-------------------|
| Define | 10% |
| Measure | 20% |
| Analyze | 25% |
| Improve | 20% |
| Control | 10% |
| Lean | 10% |
| Enterprise/Leadership | 5% |

### Preparation

- Review all training materials
- Practice statistical calculations
- Complete practice exams
- Study industry body of knowledge (ASQ, IASSC)
