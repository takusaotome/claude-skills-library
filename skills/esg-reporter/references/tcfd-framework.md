# TCFD Framework Reference Guide

## Overview

The Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) provides a framework for organizations to disclose climate-related risks and opportunities. As of 2024, TCFD recommendations are being superseded by ISSB standards (IFRS S2), but remain widely used.

## Detailed Disclosure Guidance

### Pillar 1: Governance

**Recommendation a) Board Oversight**

Describe the board's oversight of climate-related risks and opportunities.

Key disclosure points:
- Which board committee oversees climate (e.g., Sustainability Committee, Risk Committee)
- Frequency of climate discussions at board level
- How the board is informed about climate issues
- Board expertise on climate matters
- How board considers climate in major decisions

**Example disclosure**:
> "The Board's Sustainability Committee, comprising 4 independent directors, meets quarterly to oversee climate strategy. In 2024, the Committee reviewed scenario analysis results, approved our SBTi commitment, and allocated $50M for renewable energy projects."

**Recommendation b) Management's Role**

Describe management's role in assessing and managing climate-related risks and opportunities.

Key disclosure points:
- Which executives have climate responsibilities (CSO, CRO, etc.)
- Management committees addressing climate (Climate Steering Committee)
- How management monitors climate risks
- Reporting lines for climate-related issues

### Pillar 2: Strategy

**Recommendation a) Risks and Opportunities**

Describe climate-related risks and opportunities over short, medium, and long term.

**Time Horizons**:
| Horizon | Typical Definition | Risk Types |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| Short-term | 0-3 years | Current regulation, extreme weather, market shifts |
| Medium-term | 3-10 years | New policies, technology shifts, changing demand |
| Long-term | 10-30+ years | Sea level rise, structural market changes |

**Risk Categories**:

*Transition Risks* (from moving to low-carbon economy):
- **Policy & Legal**: Carbon pricing, emissions regulations, litigation
- **Technology**: Disruption, transition costs, failed investments
- **Market**: Changing customer preferences, commodity prices
- **Reputation**: Stakeholder perception, sector stigmatization

*Physical Risks* (from climate change itself):
- **Acute**: Hurricanes, floods, wildfires, extreme heat events
- **Chronic**: Sea level rise, changing precipitation, rising temperatures

**Opportunities**:
- Resource efficiency (energy, water, materials)
- Energy sources (low-carbon, renewables)
- Products & services (low-carbon products, climate solutions)
- Markets (new markets, public sector incentives)
- Resilience (supply chain, climate adaptation)

**Recommendation b) Impact on Strategy**

Describe impact on business, strategy, and financial planning.

Key areas:
- Products and services
- Supply chain / value chain
- Adaptation and mitigation activities
- Investment in R&D
- Operations (facilities, activities)
- Financial planning (capital allocation, revenue impacts)

**Recommendation c) Scenario Resilience**

Describe resilience of strategy under different climate scenarios.

**Common Scenarios**:
| Scenario | Warming | Source | Key Assumptions |
|----------|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Net Zero 2050 | 1.5°C | IEA NZE | Aggressive decarbonization, high carbon prices |
| Sustainable Development | 1.8°C | IEA SDS | Strong policies, significant clean tech adoption |
| Stated Policies | 2.7°C | IEA STEPS | Current policies continue |
| High Physical Risk | 3-4°C | IPCC RCP8.5 | Limited mitigation, severe physical impacts |

**Scenario Analysis Steps**:
1. Define scenarios (at least 2°C or lower required)
2. Evaluate business impacts under each scenario
3. Quantify financial implications where possible
4. Assess strategic resilience
5. Identify vulnerabilities and adaptation needs

### Pillar 3: Risk Management

**Recommendation a) Identifying Risks**

Describe processes for identifying and assessing climate risks.

Key elements:
- Risk identification methods (workshops, external data, stakeholder input)
- Risk assessment criteria (likelihood, impact, time horizon)
- Which assets/activities are in scope
- How risks are prioritized

**Recommendation b) Managing Risks**

Describe processes for managing climate risks.

Key elements:
- Mitigation strategies (emissions reduction, adaptation)
- Risk transfer mechanisms (insurance)
- Capital allocation for risk mitigation
- Monitoring and escalation processes

**Recommendation c) Integration into ERM**

Describe how climate risk integrates into overall risk management.

Key elements:
- Climate risks in enterprise risk register
- Risk appetite for climate exposures
- Governance and reporting integration
- Risk committee oversight

### Pillar 4: Metrics and Targets

**Recommendation a) Metrics Used**

Disclose metrics used to assess climate risks and opportunities.

**Recommended Metrics**:
| Category | Metric | Unit |
|----------|--------|------|
| GHG Emissions | Scope 1, 2, 3 | tCO2e |
| Emissions Intensity | Per revenue, per unit | tCO2e/$M, tCO2e/unit |
| Energy | Total consumption, % renewable | MWh, % |
| Water | Withdrawal, consumption | m³ |
| Physical Risk | % assets in high-risk areas | % |
| Transition Risk | Carbon price exposure | $ |
| Financial | Low-carbon revenue, green capex | $, % |

**Recommendation b) Scope 1, 2, 3 Emissions**

Disclose GHG emissions and related risks.

**Reporting Requirements**:
- Scope 1: Direct emissions (owned/controlled sources)
- Scope 2: Indirect energy emissions (purchased electricity, steam, heating)
  - Report both location-based AND market-based methods
- Scope 3: Value chain emissions (15 categories)
  - Report if material, or explain why not

**Scope 3 Categories**:
1. Purchased goods and services
2. Capital goods
3. Fuel- and energy-related activities
4. Upstream transportation and distribution
5. Waste generated in operations
6. Business travel
7. Employee commuting
8. Upstream leased assets
9. Downstream transportation and distribution
10. Processing of sold products
11. Use of sold products
12. End-of-life treatment of sold products
13. Downstream leased assets
14. Franchises
15. Investments

**Recommendation c) Targets and Progress**

Disclose climate targets and performance against them.

**Target Types**:
- Absolute targets (reduce emissions by X tCO2e)
- Intensity targets (reduce emissions per unit by X%)
- Science-based targets (SBTi-validated, aligned with 1.5°C or well-below 2°C)
- Net Zero targets (Scope 1+2+3, typically by 2050)

**Best Practice**:
- Set both near-term (2030) and long-term (2050) targets
- Include Scope 3 in targets
- Validate targets with SBTi
- Report annual progress against targets
- Explain underperformance and corrective actions

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Establish board-level climate oversight
- [ ] Assign management accountability (CSO or equivalent)
- [ ] Complete climate risk identification (transition + physical)
- [ ] Conduct scenario analysis (including 2°C or lower)
- [ ] Integrate climate into ERM framework
- [ ] Calculate Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions
- [ ] Set science-based targets (SBTi validation)
- [ ] Report TCFD disclosures (in annual or sustainability report)
- [ ] Obtain third-party assurance on emissions data
